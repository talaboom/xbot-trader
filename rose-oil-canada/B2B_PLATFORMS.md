# B2B platform playbook

Which B2B marketplaces to list on, in what order, and why. **TL;DR: Faire first, then Abound, skip the rest for year one.**

Not every platform is worth the listing effort. Many (Alibaba, Made-in-China, IndiaMart, Tradekey) are wholesale **sourcing** platforms where North American buyers go to find cheap overseas suppliers. Listing Bulgarian rose oil there puts you in a race against $100/kg fake product and wins nothing.

The right platforms for Rose Oil Canada are the ones where **North American retailers and formulators** discover **North American brands.** That list is short.

---

## Ranked platform list

### #1 — Faire (faire.com) — START HERE

**What it is:** the dominant B2B wholesale marketplace in North America. 600,000+ retailers (boutique shops, spas, independent grocery, online stores) shop for brands to stock. Beauty, wellness, apothecary, home goods are their biggest categories.

**Why it's right for you:**
- Canadian retailers use Faire heavily (Faire has a Toronto office and a Canadian buyer base)
- Zero acquisition cost — they send buyer traffic automatically once you're listed
- The clean-beauty and aromatherapy categories are underserved — "Bulgarian rose oil" has almost no competition on Faire right now
- Free shipping over $100, net-60 payment terms for retailers (Faire handles the credit risk) — lowers friction for new buyers
- Faire takes 25% of first-order revenue, 15% of reorders. Expensive? Yes. Worth it? Also yes, because acquisition cost elsewhere is higher.

**How to apply:**
1. Go to faire.com/apply and choose "Brand" / "I want to sell on Faire"
2. Application reviewed in 3–5 business days
3. Once approved, build your Faire shop (separate catalog from your Shopify; photos, descriptions, minimums can differ)
4. Set minimum order: start at $150 CAD (low) to encourage first-time buyer trial
5. Set wholesale price per SKU: aim for ~40–50% of your Shopify retail price
6. Use Faire's "Opening Order" discount (15%) to incentivize first buyers

**Minimum listings for decent visibility:**
- All three retail sizes (5 mL / 30 mL / 100 mL)
- Professional-grade photos (Faire is visual, boring photos get skipped)
- Detailed ingredient and story copy (buyers don't read fluff — they want: origin, certifications, shelf life, your lead time)
- Start with 5-item minimum order requirement; relax to 3 after you've seen 20 orders

**Expected velocity year 1:** 5–15 first orders, $3K–12K CAD revenue, ~2 of those will reorder, few will become quarterly buyers. Fastest way to get real customer data.

### #2 — Abound (helloabound.com)

**What it is:** direct Faire competitor, smaller catalog, slightly different retailer base (leans more gift-shop / boutique-retail vs. Faire's broader mix).

**Why add it:** duplicates most Faire work with very little extra effort. Takes ~15% commission (a bit less than Faire first-order). Sends additional incremental orders.

**When:** month 2, after Faire is running smoothly.

### #3 — Tundra (tundra.com)

**What it is:** 0% commission platform — charges buyer a small fee, brands keep full wholesale price.

**Why consider:** best margin per order if you get orders. **Why to skip in year 1:** traffic is much smaller than Faire, quality of buyers is mixed, and the platform has had stability issues (rumored funding troubles). Revisit in year 2 if they're still around.

### #4 — Amazon Business (business.amazon.ca)

**What it is:** Amazon's B2B storefront. Enterprise buyers, price-sensitive.

**Why to skip:** rose oil on Amazon is a race to the bottom dominated by $40 "100% pure rose essential oil" fakes. You will not compete on price. You will dilute your brand. **Stay off Amazon in year 1.**

### #5 — Alibaba / IndiaMart / Made-in-China / Tradekey

**Why to skip entirely:** these are B2B sourcing platforms oriented around Chinese, Indian, and Southeast Asian suppliers selling to North American importers. Listing there as a Canadian distributor does nothing useful — and buyers there assume you're the manufacturer. Your producer is in Bulgaria, not your warehouse.

### Niche platforms worth looking at opportunistically

- **Pound & Grain** (beauty-specific B2B, much smaller)
- **The Detox Market wholesale program** (if you can get approved, The Detox Market is a clean-beauty retailer with 8+ locations in Canada)
- **Credo Beauty wholesale** (US-focused, strong clean-beauty curation)
- **Beauty Heroes** (subscription box, very curated, if they feature you it's huge)
- **Shopify Collective** (if you're on Shopify Plus — lets other Shopify stores sell your products; skip until year 2)

## Faire-specific optimization

Faire's internal search and recommendation algorithm weighs a few things heavily. Worth knowing:

1. **Complete profile:** fill everything. Brand story, video if possible, press mentions, wholesale policies. Completion score affects discovery.
2. **Product tagging:** use all relevant tags (essential oil, rose, aromatherapy, clean beauty, made in Canada — yes, "made in Canada" applies even though the oil is Bulgarian because you're blending/bottling in Canada, which Faire accepts). Don't over-tag — 8–10 relevant tags per product.
3. **Opening Order minimums** — Faire surfaces brands with low opening minimums more. Start at $150 CAD, not $500.
4. **Response time:** Faire tracks how fast you reply to buyer messages. Sub-24-hour response dramatically boosts your "Responsive brand" badge.
5. **Free shipping over $X:** buyers filter by free shipping. Set free shipping threshold at $200 CAD for orders shipped within Canada.
6. **Seasonal events:** Faire runs "New Brand Month", "Market Weeks" (quarterly). Apply to be featured — they pick ~30 new brands per market week and give them homepage placement. Application is a 5-minute form; zero cost; massive exposure when selected.

## Platform rollout timeline

**Month 1** — Apply to Faire, set up shop, list all three products. Target: approved and live within 10 business days.

**Month 2** — Apply to Abound, duplicate Faire listings. Apply for Faire's next Market Week feature. Pitch The Detox Market and Credo Beauty for direct wholesale account.

**Month 3–6** — Optimize Faire listings based on buyer behavior (which SKU gets viewed most? adjust photo, headline, price). Apply to Beauty Heroes subscription box as a feature.

**Month 6+** — If Faire + Abound + direct wholesale is working, ignore other platforms. If not, reassess — maybe Amazon Business with a premium SKU at a protected price, or Tundra if they're still stable.

## Photo requirements (applies to all platforms)

Minimum asset pack to produce before listing anywhere:

- 5 product photos per SKU on white background (main listing shots)
- 5 lifestyle / in-use photos per SKU (bottle with petals, bottle in a spa setting, bottle being held, bottle with the GC-MS lab report)
- 1 brand hero image (ideally Mariana in the distillery or valley — even a stock photo of the Kazanlak valley works if budget is tight)
- 1 short video (15–30 sec) — phone footage of the oil being poured, petals being distilled (producer can provide this footage to you by asking)

**Budget:** ~$400–800 CAD for a 1-day product photo shoot at a Toronto indie studio, or free if shot at home with a window and a tripod.
