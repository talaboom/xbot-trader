# Customer target list

Three segments, ranked by closeness of fit for a first-year importer with producer-direct Bulgarian supply and a founder with pharmaceutical-industry credibility.

**How to use this list:**
1. Before contacting anyone, Google each company — confirm they're still operating, scroll their ingredient lists or wholesale page, and note any recent news (acquisition, founder change, pivot). Takes 2 minutes per company and saves you from embarrassing misfires.
2. Score each on a quick 1-5 fit rating before outreach. Don't waste samples on a 2/5.
3. Prioritize by warmth: if Mariana has *any* mutual connection (shared supplier, former colleague, LinkedIn second-degree), that company jumps to the top of the list.

Total: ~50 targets below. Realistic expectation: 10–20% reply rate on cold outreach, 50%+ on warm. Target 5–8 paying customers in year 1.

Mariana's strongest wedge. These are B2B buyers who already formulate custom topical products; rose oil slots into anti-aging serums, wound-healing compounds, aromatherapy blends. ## SEGMENT 3 — Middle Eastern / South Asian grocery + bakery channel (high volume, lower margin)

This segment buys **rose water (hydrosol)**, not rose oil — but the supply chain is the same, it's a natural product-line extension. Lower margin (maybe 15-25% instead of 50%+) but much higher volume and fast repeat buyers. Worth adding as a second SKU once the main business is running.

## Priority batch for first 30 samples

Aim for a mix: 10 compounding pharmacies (warm), 15 indie clean-beauty (cold), 5 indie perfumers (cold). Don't send to grocery channel yet — they're for rose water phase 2.

Sample kit: 1 mL amber glass vial of the oil + printed GC-MS report + one-pager spec sheet + price list (5 mL / 30 mL / 100 mL / 1 kg).

Cost per sample kit: ~$25 CAD materials + shipping. 30 samples = ~$750 all-in. Break-even on 1 closed 30-mL order.
