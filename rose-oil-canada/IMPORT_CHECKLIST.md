# Canadian import checklist

Regulatory and operational steps required to legally import Bulgarian rose oil into Canada and sell it to Canadian customers in the three channels (compounding pharmacies, cosmetics brands, retail DTC).

**Budget realistic expectation:** $8,000–12,000 CAD in year-one setup fees (incorporation + insurance + first product licences + first storage lease). Most recurring after year 1 is under $3,000/year until volume scales.

**Don't let regulatory paralysis stop you from selling.** The reality is many indie importers start shipping samples before every licence is formally approved, and nothing bad happens until they hit retail volume. That said, for compounding-pharmacy sales and any therapeutic claims, licences matter from day one.

---

## 1. Business formation

**Entity type:** Ontario numbered corporation is fine. You can file a trade name ("Rose Oil Canada") separately for branding.

**Steps:**
- File Articles of Incorporation with Ontario Ministry of Public and Business Service Delivery (online via ServiceOntario, ~$300 CAD)
- Get federal Business Number (BN) from CRA (free, online)
- Register for HST/GST **immediately** — you'll be above the $30K/year threshold within months and you want to recover input tax credits from day one
- Open business bank account (RBC, TD, BMO all have startup offers; $20–40/mo)

**Cost:** ~$500 CAD total including incorporation fees.

---

## 2. CBSA import basics (HS code + CETA)

**HS code for essential oils of rose:** `3301.29.00`

**Tariff under CETA:** **0%** (Bulgaria is an EU member state; the Canada-EU Comprehensive Economic and Trade Agreement eliminates tariffs on essential oils of EU origin).

To claim CETA treatment:
- Supplier must issue a **CETA origin declaration** on the invoice or a separate certificate. Sample wording:
  > *"The exporter of the products covered by this document declares that, except where otherwise clearly indicated, these products are of EU preferential origin."*
- Keep all origin documentation for 7 years (CBSA audit window).

**Customs broker:** hire one for first 5 shipments minimum. Recommended: Livingston International, Cole International, A&A Customs Brokers. Fees: $50–100 per shipment. They'll handle the B3 customs entry, CETA claim, and CBSA interaction.

**Importing 101 resources:**
- CBSA Step-by-Step Guide: https://www.cbsa-asfc.gc.ca/import/menu-eng.html
- Trade Commissioner Service (free advisory for Canadian importers): https://www.tradecommissioner.gc.ca

## 3. Product licensing — pick the right path for each use case

Rose oil crosses three regulatory categories in Canada. You probably need **all three** eventually, but stage them based on which customer you're selling to first.

### A. Natural Health Product (NHP) — for any therapeutic / aromatherapy claim

If you claim the oil "promotes relaxation", "supports skin health", etc., you need a **Natural Product Number (NPN)** issued by Health Canada's Natural and Non-prescription Health Products Directorate (NNHPD).

- Application: NHP Online Solution portal (free submission)
- Typical approval: 60–180 days, depending on class (Class I simplest)
- Fee: varies, usually $450–1,900 CAD per product licence
- **Plan for this starting month 2–3.** You can sell without an NPN if you make zero therapeutic claims on labels / website, but it narrows your copy significantly.

### B. Cosmetic — for any cosmetic-category sale

If sold for cosmetic use (skin application for aesthetics without therapeutic claim), file a **Cosmetic Notification Form (CNF)** with Health Canada:
- Online: https://www.canada.ca/en/health-canada/services/consumer-product-safety/cosmetics/notify-health-canada.html
- Notification is required within **10 days of first sale in Canada**
- No fee
- Takes ~30 minutes to file per product/fragrance

**Do this from day one** — it's free and quick. Skipping it is the most common enforcement action Health Canada takes against indie brands.

### C. Food grade (rose WATER, not rose OIL) — CFIA

If you expand into rose water for bakeries/grocery, register for **Safe Food for Canadians Regulations (SFCR)** via the CFIA's My CFIA portal:
- SFCR licence required to import/sell food in Canada
- Fee: $250 CAD/year base licence
- Food-grade lab analysis required for first shipment
- **Stage this for phase 2** (month 6+) when rose water becomes a line

## 4. Labels and packaging compliance

For any product sold in Canada:

- **Bilingual labelling** (English + French) required by Consumer Packaging and Labelling Act
- Mandatory info on label: common product name, net quantity (metric), name and address of Canadian distributor, country of origin
- INCI (International Nomenclature of Cosmetic Ingredients) name on cosmetic products: `Rosa Damascena Flower Oil`
- For NHPs: NPN number on label, recommended dose, risk information

**Labelling service recommendation:** use a freelance label designer familiar with Canadian requirements ($300–600 per SKU). A mistake on a label can trigger a recall.

---

## 5. Insurance

**Product liability insurance** is mandatory if you sell to any pharmacy, spa, or retailer — they'll require a certificate of insurance with $2M or $5M per-occurrence coverage naming them as additional insured.

- Typical cost: $1,500–2,500/year for $2M coverage on a first-year importer
- Providers: Northbridge, Intact, Aviva, or use a broker like ALIGNED Insurance
- Also consider: general liability ($500/year), cyber liability if running Shopify ($400/year)

## 6. Storage & fulfilment

Rose oil is **light- and heat-sensitive.** Degrades fast above 22°C or in direct light. Needs:

- Cool storage (10–18°C ideal)
- Dark — amber glass or aluminum drum storage, or a dark room
- Dry (under 50% humidity)

**Options in Mississauga:**

1. **Rent a small climate-controlled unit:** Storwell, Public Storage, or Guardian Storage have 5×5 or 5×10 climate-controlled units for $150–300/month. Fine for first year under 20 kg inventory.
2. **3PL (third-party logistics):** Kuehne+Nagel Canada, DHL Supply Chain, or a smaller GTA 3PL like Maple Logistics. ~$400–800/month for a pallet + fulfilment. Worth it at 50+ kg or heavy Shopify order volume.
3. **Home/garage storage:** technically allowed for up to 1 kg personal inventory, not once you have HST-registered business doing B2B. Don't.

## 7. Testing partners

You want **two** independent labs: one in Europe (closer to producer), one in Canada (closer to customer).

**Europe:**
- Phytocontrol (France) — specialists in essential oil GC-MS, ~€150–300 per panel
- Alpha Lab (Bulgaria) — local, cheaper, faster turnaround
- Your producer may offer tested reports — **always request an independent one anyway, at your cost, for your records**

**Canada:**
- Bureau Veritas Canada (Mississauga office) — widely respected
- Maxxam Analytics (SGS group) — similar
- Cost: $400–600 CAD per GC-MS panel

**Frequency:** one EU-side COA per lot ex-producer + one Canadian re-verification per year (or every 3rd lot, whichever is more frequent).

## 8. First 30 days — operational task list

- [ ] Incorporate Ontario corp
- [ ] Get BN from CRA, register for HST
- [ ] Open business bank account
- [ ] Request product liability insurance quote and bind policy
- [ ] Secure climate-controlled storage (even 5x5 unit is enough for year 1)
- [ ] Identify Canadian customs broker, open a relationship account
- [ ] Negotiate first producer order: 1 kg, request CETA origin declaration + EU GC-MS COA
- [ ] Contract Canadian lab (Bureau Veritas Mississauga most common) for re-verification
- [ ] File Cosmetic Notification Forms (CNFs) for each SKU you'll sell
- [ ] Begin NHP (NPN) application for primary SKU
- [ ] Engage label designer for bilingual English/French label compliance
- [ ] Set up label printer (local print shop or sticker service like StickerYou)

## 9. Common mistakes to avoid

- **Skipping HST registration early.** You must register once revenue exceeds $30K in any 12-month period; better to register day one and claim all input tax credits on setup expenses.
- **Selling with therapeutic claims but no NPN.** Health Canada enforces this. Don't put "calming" or "anti-inflammatory" on a label until the NPN is approved.
- **Storing product above 22°C.** A summer heatwave in a non-climate-controlled garage will degrade rose oil's aromatic compounds — your GC-MS will fail when you test the next batch.
- **Accepting a supplier's COA at face value.** Always re-verify the first lot from a new producer, independently, in Canada. Even trusted producers have been caught adulterating when the harvest is weak.
- **Discounting to match Alibaba prices.** You're not selling the same product. Adulterated rose oil on Alibaba is $500/kg because it isn't rose oil. Don't race that market.
