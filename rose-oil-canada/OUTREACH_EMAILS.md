# Outreach emails

Three segments, three different voices. **Do not reuse the same template across segments** — compounding pharmacists expect formal credentials, indie beauty founders expect founder-to-founder warmth, grocery importers expect price and terms. Mixing the voices makes you sound generic and kills reply rate.

**General rules:**
- Every email gets a personalized first line referencing something specific to them (recent product launch, ingredient they already use, their city). Three minutes of LinkedIn / website scrolling per target.
- Subject lines under 60 characters. No emoji, no ALL CAPS, no "re:" fake-reply tricks.
- Include **one** specific number (price, purity, harvest year) — concrete beats vague.
- Attach the GC-MS one-pager as a PDF. Not a 10-page deck.
- Sign with Mariana's name + title + credential (former president, Cami Pharmaceutical).
- Send Tuesday–Thursday, 9:30–11 AM ET. Avoid Monday morning and Friday afternoon.

## Follow-up cadence

If no reply in 7 days, send one follow-up only. Don't chase three times — it kills future rapport.

**Follow-up template (universal):**

Subject: line — prepend "Following up: " to original

## What to track

Simple spreadsheet — 10 columns, nothing fancy:

| Company | Contact | Email | Segment | Sent date | Replied? | Sample shipped? | Order? | $ | Notes |

Aim for these conversion rates in year 1:
- Sent → replied: 15–25% (cold) / 50%+ (warm)
- Replied → sample shipped: 60%
- Sample → first order: 25–35%

So 30 cold emails → ~6 replies → ~4 samples shipped → ~1 paying customer. 30 cold emails per month = 12 paying B2B customers in year 1. Realistic, not aspirational.
