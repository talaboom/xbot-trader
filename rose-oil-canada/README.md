# Rose Oil Canada

Importer / distributor of authentic Bulgarian rose oil (*Rosa damascena*) from direct-producer relationships in the Kazanlak Rose Valley, targeting Canadian compounding pharmacies, indie clean-beauty brands, and premium retail.

## Why this business, specifically

- **Bulgaria produces ~70% of the world's true rose oil.** The Kazanlak Valley has been the centre of the global market for 400 years.
- **Price economics are real:** producer-direct wholesale is ~$7,500–9,500 CAD/kg. Retail repackaged in 5 mL bottles sells for the equivalent of $24,000+ CAD/kg. Gross margin after all import and fulfilment costs is 30–80% depending on channel.
- **Two unfair advantages:**
  1. Mariana (founder) has direct producer connections — Bulgarian-born, family ties to the distilleries.
  2. Mariana's background as former president of Cami Pharmaceutical (Mississauga, Ontario) means warm introductions to Canadian compounding pharmacies and cosmeceutical formulators — exactly the people who buy rose oil in bulk.
- **Bulgarian rose oil is EU-origin**, so under the Canada-EU Comprehensive Economic and Trade Agreement (CETA) it enters Canada **duty-free** (HS 3301.29). Saves 5-6% vs. most other origins.

## Market reality check

- Rose oil is **heavily adulterated.** Maybe 80% of what's sold online as "Bulgarian rose oil" is geranium oil cut with synthetic linalool. This is our single biggest opportunity: provide a **GC-MS certificate of analysis per batch** and a **Protected Designation of Origin (PDO) certificate** where possible, and you beat 95% of competitors on the only metric serious buyers care about.
- The fragrance industry is dominated by four global houses (Givaudan, Firmenich, IFF, Symrise). We are **not** chasing them. They buy tonnes per year direct from established distillers on 20-year relationships. Our customers are the 30-to-500 kg annual buyer range — compounding pharmacies, indie brands, aromatherapy retailers.

## Economics at landed cost

| Cost line | Per kg (CAD) |
|---|---|
| Farmgate from distiller (PDO-certified, first press) | $7,500–9,500 |
| Air freight + temperature control | $300–500 |
| Customs broker + CBSA entry | $50–100 (amortized) |
| Canadian GC-MS re-verification | $100–200 (amortized) |
| Insurance + Mississauga cold storage | $100–200 |
| **Landed cost in Mississauga** | **~$9,500–10,500 CAD/kg** |

## 90-day roadmap

**Days 1–14 — foundations**
- Incorporate Ontario numbered company (or trade name "Rose Oil Canada")
- Register HST/GST (mandatory over $30K revenue, do it immediately)
- Product liability insurance quote (~$1,500–2,500/year typical)
- Lock in Bulgarian producer — negotiate 10-kg annual minimum for locked pricing
- Courier 1 kg sample to Mississauga for Canadian-side GC-MS verification
- Set up Mississauga cold/dark storage (a 3PL like Kuehne+Nagel, or rent a small temperature-controlled unit)

**Days 15–45 — market entry**
- Launch Shopify store (`WEBSITE_PLAN.md`)
- Open Faire brand account (`B2B_PLATFORMS.md`)
- Send 20 warm-intro samples via Mariana's pharmacy network
- Send 30 cold samples to indie beauty brands from `CUSTOMERS.md`
- Health Canada Cosmetic Notification Form filed for retail bottles
- Health Canada NHP licence filed for therapeutic claims

**Days 46–90 — first orders**
- Convert sample recipients into purchase orders (1-kg bulk or 30 mL qty orders)
- First retail bottle sales via Shopify
- Attend one trade show (CHFA East in Toronto, Sept / Naturally Canada)
- Reorder inventory — target 4 kg for year 1
